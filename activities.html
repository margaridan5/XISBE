Speakers: Gabriela Ribeiro, Sílvia F. Henriques

Gabriela Ribeiro

Gabriela Ribeiro é Professora Auxiliar na NOVA Medical School (Universidade NOVA de Lisboa) e investigadora no Comprehensive Health Research Center (CHRC). É doutorada em Neurociências pela Faculdade de Medicina da Universidade de Lisboa (FMUL), tendo desenvolvido o seu trabalho no Champalimaud Research & Clinical Centre, onde investigou os mecanismos neurais e comportamentais associados à obesidade. A sua formação integra ainda uma licenciatura em Ciências da Nutrição pela Universidade do Porto e um mestrado em Doenças Metabólicas e Comportamento Alimentar pela FMUL.

Ao longo do seu percurso, desenvolveu trabalho em contextos internacionais, incluindo o APC Microbiome Ireland (University College Cork), explorando o eixo intestino–cérebro e o papel da alimentação na função cerebral e cognitiva.

Atualmente, a sua investigação foca-se na forma como o estado metabólico influencia a perceção sensorial e os processos de recompensa alimentar, com o objetivo de identificar marcadores precoces relevantes para a obesidade para as doenças do comportamento alimentar. Para além da atividade científica, é também nutricionista clínica, âmbito no qual faz a translação da evidência científica para a terapia médico-nutricional dirigida a pessoas com doenças psiquiátricas.

Sílvia F. Henriques

Sílvia F. Henriques is a biochemist and microbiologist studying how nutrition and the microbiome interact to regulate metabolism, physiology, behavior, and aging. She obtained her PhD from Instituto Superior Técnico, where she investigated yeast metabolism and stress adaptation, before shifting to host–microbe interactions using Drosophila melanogaster as a model system. She is currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Champalimaud Foundation (Behavior and Metabolism Laboratory). Her work integrates dietary and microbial manipulations with metabolomics, transcriptomics, genetics, and behavioral quantitative assays to uncover conserved mechanisms linking nutrition, microbial ecology, and host health.

At the Edge of Life: Astrobiology and the Search for the (Im)Possible

Tuesday, 11:30 - 13:00

📍 TBD

Speakers: Zita Martins, Daniela Pinto, Francisco Santos

Moderadora: Leonilde Moreira

Zita Martins

Zita Martins is an Astrobiologist, Associate Professor with Aggregation and Vice-President for International Affairs at Instituto Superior Técnico (IST). Her research interests include the detection of chemical signatures of extra-terrestrial life on space missions, and the potential chemical contribution of meteorites and comets to the origin of life.

She graduated in Chemistry from IST in 2002 and obtained her PhD in Astrobiology from Leiden University (The Netherlands) in 2007. She was an Invited Scientist at NASA Goddard (USA) in 2005 and 2006. She worked at Imperial College London from 2007 to 2017, where in 2009 she was awarded a University Research Fellowship worth 1 million pounds by the Royal Society. Since January 2018 she is a Professor at IST, where she created the first Astrobiology and Cosmochemistry laboratory in Portugal.

Zita Martins is co-investigator on ESA projects OREOcube and EXOcube aboard the International Space Station, a Community Scientist on the ARIEL mission (ESA), a member of the Hayabusa2 insoluble organic matter team (JAXA), and a member of the Comet Interceptor mission (ESA). In 2015 she was appointed Officer of the Military Order of Saint James of the Sword by the President of Portugal, and in 2025 was appointed Grand-Officer of the Order of Public Instruction.

Daniela Pinto

Daniela Pinto é Investigadora Auxiliar no iMed.ULisboa (Faculdade de Farmácia da Universidade de Lisboa), onde coordena o projeto PROSPERUS, focado na engenharia de probióticos para a produção e entrega de proteínas terapêuticas. Licenciada em Biologia Microbiana e Genética e Doutorada em Biologia (Microbiologia) pela Faculdade de Ciências da Universidade de Lisboa, o seu percurso académico é marcado por uma forte componente internacional. Desenvolveu investigação pós-doutoral na Ludwig-Maximilians-Universität München e na Technische Universität Dresden, na Alemanha, através de uma prestigiada bolsa Marie Curie, e realizou estadias de investigação em instituições de referência como a Newcastle University e a Københavns Universitet.

Francisco Santos

Francisco Santos is an orthopaedic surgery resident with a focused interest in aerospace medicine. Since 2021, he has been a researcher at CEMA, working on topics such as Spaceflight-Associated Neuro-ocular Syndrome (SANS) and intracranial pressure determination in microgravity. He has also contributed to research in AI-assisted ultrasound labeling. His work has been presented at multiple conferences, including the Aerospace Medical Association (AsMA) Annual Scientific Meeting and national meetings in Portugal.

Leonilde Moreira (Moderadora)

Leonilde Moreira is an Associate Professor in the Department of Bioengineering at Instituto Superior Técnico, coordinator of the Master's in Biotechnology, and Principal Investigator at the Institute for Bioengineering and Biosciences. Her scientific work focuses on molecular microbiology, bacterial genomics, and host-microorganism interactions, with emphasis on the adaptation, evolution, and persistence of microbial communities in complex environments. She teaches course units such as Genetic Engineering and Molecular Biotechnology, training students in microbiology, bioengineering, and biotechnology.

Genetic Footprints of Portugal: History in Our DNA

Wednesday, 14:00 - 15:30

📍 TBD

Speakers: Luísa Mesquita Pereira, João Tiago Brochado de Almeida

Moderadora: Joana Feliciano

Luísa Mesquita Pereira

Luísa Pereira has a degree in Biology, and a Master's and PhD in Human Population Genetics. She is a group leader at i3S (Institute of Research and Innovation in Health, University of Porto), interested in using genetics to infer the past and evolution of human populations, as well as to evaluate susceptibility of human populations to complex diseases. She has mapped genes that may protect sub-Saharan African populations from presenting worse clinical phenotypes following infection by Helicobacter pylori and dengue fever virus.

Through international collaborations with anthropologists, geneticists, and clinicians, she has generated knowledge about the genetic diversity of populations across all continents. She is a regular presence in the media, organizes outreach events for the general public — including the course "Genetic Odyssey" — and is co-author of the popular science book The Portuguese Genetic Heritage: Human History Preserved in Genes.

João Tiago Brochado de Almeida

João Tiago Brochado de Almeida is a PhD candidate in Molecular and Environmental Biology at the University of Minho and University of Huddersfield, where he focuses on ancient DNA and human population genetics. His current research investigates the genomic history of Eurasian populations from the Bronze Age to the Medieval period, with particular emphasis on ancient individuals from Romania. By integrating high-throughput sequencing with advanced computational analyses, his work aims to reconstruct patterns of migration, admixture, and demographic change across key moments of human history.

He has contributed to studies on human population history in São Tomé and Príncipe, exploring the genetic legacy of slavery and ethnogenesis in the Gulf of Guinea, and has worked in cancer genomics analyzing mitochondrial DNA heteroplasmy. He is author and co-author of several peer-reviewed publications in international journals spanning ancient DNA authenticity, human population history, and genomic variation.

Joana Feliciano (Moderadora)

Joana Feliciano is a researcher at the Institute of Bioengineering and Biosciences (iBB) and an Invited Assistant Professor at Instituto Superior Técnico. She earned her PhD in Biotechnology and Biosciences from IST in 2017, focusing on the virulence mechanisms of opportunistic pathogens from the Burkholderia cepacia complex. She completed international research stays at the University of Montpellier, the University of Würzburg, and the University of Zurich.

Currently, she investigates how regulatory RNAs and outer membrane vesicles contribute to antimicrobial resistance in clinically relevant opportunistic pathogens, aiming to identify new molecular targets for innovative antimicrobial strategies.

Miguel Amador

Laser Written Bioelectronics: Building Blocks for Epidermal Sensors

Monday, 11:30 - 13:00

Speaker: Tomás Pinheiro

Tomás Pinheiro

Tomás Pinheiro is a researcher working at the intersection of laser materials processing, flexible electronics, and wearable bioengineering. With a background in Biomedical Engineering and a research trajectory spanning NOVA School of Science and Technology, KAUST, and CENIMAT|i3N, he has built a distinctive expertise in Direct Laser Writing (DLW) as a sustainable, versatile platform for creating next-generation bioelectronic materials and devices. His work has introduced new methodologies for laser-engineered soft conductors, including laser-induced graphene on unconventional substrates, peel-off transfer techniques for skin-conformable electrodes, and multimaterial laser synthesis strategies for sensing and energy applications.

Throughout his PhD and postdoctoral research, Tomás has contributed to high-impact publications in journals such as Advanced Materials, ACS Nano, Materials Horizons, and Applied Physics Reviews. His work bridges fundamental materials science with practical device engineering, always with a focus on accessibility, sustainability, and real-world biomedical relevance.

Workshop description: Wearable biosensing is rapidly transforming health monitoring, sports performance, and human–machine interfaces. Participants will explore how laser-based fabrication enables rapid, low-cost production of flexible bioelectronic components for epidermal sensors. After a short introductory presentation, students will rotate through five practical modules:

Module 1: Laser synthesis of electrode conductive materials.
Module 2: Electrochemical glucose sensing.
Module 3: Epifluidics module for sweat transportation.
Module 4: Carbon bioelectrodes for physiological monitoring.
Module 5: Iontophoresis module for sweat stimulation.

Sandra Simões

Sandra Simões is a Principal Investigator at the Research Institute for Medicines (iMed), Faculty of Pharmacy, University of Lisbon. Tease Your Thesis

Thursday, 11:30 - 13:00

Speakers: Rebeca Friedrich, Teresa Porqueddu

Rebeca Friedrich

Rebeca Friedrich completed both her BSc and MSc in Biomedical Engineering at Instituto Superior Técnico. Throughout her academic path, she developed experience in biomechanics and medical device development through internships in distinct research environments.

During her BSc thesis at iBB, she contributed to the design and fabrication of 3D-printed gradient scaffolds for osteochondral tissue engineering. She later completed an internship at the Laboratory of Biomechanics and Functional Morphology of the Faculty of Human Kinetics.

In her MSc thesis, carried out at the Department of Mechanical Engineering, she investigated the biomechanical impact of knee arthroplasty reconstruction surgery using a modular megaprosthesis in oncological patients, focusing on lower-limb biomechanics after surgical intervention.

Teresa Porqueddu

Teresa Porqueddu recently completed her Master's degree in Biomedical Engineering, following the Tissue Engineering and Regenerative Medicine profile. During her academic training, she expanded her international experience through Erasmus at TH Lübeck and an Erasmus Internship at the Macromolecular Engineering Laboratory at ETH Zurich, where she developed both her PIC 2 project and her Master's thesis.

Her thesis focused on an in vitro tumour invasion model, with particular emphasis on optimizing its cellular and structural components. The work explored the activation and role of cancer-associated fibroblasts, as well as changes in the viscoelastic properties of the hydrogel matrix used in the system.

Previously, during her undergraduate studies, she carried out her PIC 1 at GIMM in the Luísa Figueiredo Lab – Biology of Parasitism, where she worked on adapting a microfluidic model to replicate bovine brain microvasculature and study the sequestration of trypanosomes to endothelial cells.
